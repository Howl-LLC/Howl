Howl Copyright and IP Policy

Effective Date: March 1, 2026 · Last Updated: June 17, 2026

Howl respects the intellectual property rights of others and expects users to do the same. This policy describes how to report copyright and trademark infringement on the Howl platform (the "Service") pursuant to the Digital Millennium Copyright Act (17 U.S.C. § 512), the Lanham Act, and equivalent international frameworks.

1. Designated Agent

Howl DMCA Agent
Email: support@howlpro.com (subject: "DMCA Takedown Request" or "DMCA Counter-Notification")
Mailing Address: Howl LLC, 30 N Gould St Ste N, Sheridan, WY 82801, USA.

2. Filing a Copyright Takedown Notice

If you believe that content on the Service infringes your copyright, send a written notice to our designated agent containing all of the following:

  1. Identification of the copyrighted work you claim is infringed (or a representative list if multiple works are involved).
  2. Identification of the infringing material and information reasonably sufficient to permit us to locate it (for example, server name, channel name, message link or ID, URL of the content).
  3. Your contact information: name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address.
  4. A statement that you have a good-faith belief that use of the material in the manner complained of is not authorized by the copyright owner, its agent, or the law.
  5. A statement, under penalty of perjury, that the information in the notice is accurate and that you are authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner.
  6. Your physical or electronic signature (or the signature of a person authorized to act on behalf of the copyright owner).

Send copyright takedown notices to support@howlpro.com with the subject line "DMCA Takedown Request".

Notices must be submitted in English. If your notice is in a different language, include a certified or notarized English translation.

Misrepresentation warning. Any person who knowingly materially misrepresents that content is infringing is liable for damages, including costs and attorney's fees, under 17 U.S.C. § 512(f). Repeat abusive or bad-faith notices may be blocked from the reporting process and reported to the relevant authorities. Consider consulting an attorney before filing if you are unsure whether the use constitutes infringement.

3. How We Process Takedown Notices

Upon receiving a valid takedown notice:

  1. We will promptly remove or disable access to the allegedly infringing material.
  2. We will notify the user who posted the content that it has been removed and provide a copy of the notice (with the reporter's personal contact information redacted where permitted).
  3. We will retain a record of the notice for legal compliance.

4. Counter-Notification

If you believe your content was removed by mistake or misidentification, you may file a counter-notification with our designated agent containing all of the following:

  1. Identification of the material that was removed and its location before removal.
  2. A statement, under penalty of perjury, that you have a good-faith belief the material was removed or disabled as a result of mistake or misidentification.
  3. Your name, address, and telephone number.
  4. A statement that you consent to the jurisdiction of the federal court in your district (or, if outside the United States, any judicial district in which Howl may be found), and that you will accept service of process from the person who filed the original notice or their agent.
  5. Your physical or electronic signature.

Send counter-notifications to support@howlpro.com with the subject line "DMCA Counter-Notification". Counter-notifications must be submitted in English or accompanied by a certified English translation.

Upon receiving a valid counter-notification:

  1. We will forward a copy to the original complainant.
  2. If the original complainant does not file a court action within 10 to 14 business days, we will restore the removed content.

Knowingly material misrepresentation in a counter-notification is subject to the same liability under 17 U.S.C. § 512(f) described in Section 2.

5. Repeat Infringer Policy

Howl maintains a policy of terminating, in appropriate circumstances, the accounts of users who are repeat infringers. Specifically:

  • Users who receive three valid DMCA takedown notices within a 12-month period will have their account permanently terminated.
  • We may terminate accounts with fewer strikes if the infringement is egregious or willful.
  • Counter-notifications that result in content restoration will remove the associated strike.

6. Trademark Claims

If you believe content on the Service infringes your trademark or service mark, send a written notice to support@howlpro.com with the subject line "Trademark Claim" and include:

  1. Description of the trademark and its registration or application number (if any).
  2. Description of the allegedly infringing use and its location on the Service (for example, server name, channel name, message link, URL).
  3. A good-faith statement that the use is not authorized by the trademark owner or the law.
  4. Your name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address.
  5. Your physical or electronic signature.

We evaluate trademark claims against our Community Guidelines, applicable law, and relevant trademark rights. We may remove or restrict content that infringes a trademark, request additional information, or decline to act if the claim is insufficient. Trademark complainants should consider whether a non-infringing use (such as nominative fair use, commentary, or parody) applies before submitting a claim.

7. Right of Publicity and Unauthorized Likeness

If your name, likeness, image, voice, or other identifying characteristics have been used on the Service without authorization (including in AI-generated or manipulated content such as deepfakes), send a written notice to support@howlpro.com with the subject line "Right of Publicity Claim" and include:

  1. Description of the identifying characteristic used (for example, name, image, likeness, voice) and, if applicable, the jurisdiction whose right-of-publicity law you rely on (for example, California Civil Code § 3344, New York Civil Rights Law §§ 50 to 51, or the Tennessee ELVIS Act).
  2. Identification of the allegedly infringing content and its location on the Service (server, channel, message link, URL).
  3. Your name, mailing address, telephone number, and email address.
  4. A good-faith statement that the use is not authorized and does not fall within an applicable exception (for example, news reporting, commentary, or parody).
  5. Your physical or electronic signature.

We evaluate right-of-publicity claims against applicable law, our Community Guidelines, and user expression rights. Resolution may include content removal or account restrictions, subject to applicable law and user appeal rights under our Terms of Service.

8. Copyright-Bypass Tools and Piracy Facilitation

Operating or participating in spaces on the Service that distribute, promote, or facilitate the following is a violation of our Community Guidelines and these policies, and may result in content removal and account termination:

  • Unauthorized copies of copyrighted works (films, television programs, music, books, games, or software).
  • Tools, services, or instructions whose primary purpose is to circumvent copyright protection (DRM bypasses, account-sharing services for paid platforms, credential-sharing tools).
  • Game exploits, cheats, or modifications that violate the publisher's terms of service and are distributed for profit or at scale.

Report such content to support@howlpro.com with the subject line "Copyright Report", or through in-app reporting.

9. DMCA Subpoenas

Copyright holders seeking to identify an alleged infringer may obtain a subpoena from the clerk of any United States district court under 17 U.S.C. § 512(h). Howl processes valid § 512(h) subpoenas for user identification through our Law Enforcement Guidelines.

Send § 512(h) subpoenas to support@howlpro.com with the subject line "DMCA Subpoena". The submission requirements in our Law Enforcement Guidelines apply, including non-editable PDF format, identifying information for the user account, and the internal reference number we assign on receipt.

10. Limitations

  • End-to-end encrypted content. Direct messages and group direct messages on Howl are end-to-end encrypted. We do not have access to the plaintext of end-to-end encrypted messages to evaluate infringement claims. Takedown requests for DM content require sufficient identifying information for us to act on the specific material.
  • Non-US users. If you are outside the United States, local copyright or trademark laws may apply. We process notices in good faith under the DMCA and cooperate with equivalent processes under applicable law (for example, the EU Copyright Directive, the UK CDPA).
  • Government takedown requests. Content takedown requests from law enforcement or government authorities (including EU DSA Article 9 removal orders and UK Online Safety Act trusted-flagger reports) are handled under our Law Enforcement Guidelines.
