Howl Accessibility Statement

Effective Date: June 16, 2026 · Last Updated: June 17, 2026

Howl LLC ("Howl," "we," "us," "our") is committed to making the Howl platform usable by as many people as possible, including people who use assistive technologies. This statement explains the accessibility standard we work toward, what we have done so far, where we know we fall short, and how to reach us if you run into a barrier.

1. Our Commitment

Accessibility is part of how we build Howl, not an afterthought. We aim to provide an experience that is perceivable, operable, understandable, and robust for everyone, and we treat accessibility feedback as a priority when planning fixes and new features.

2. Conformance Status

We aim to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.2, Level AA, published by the World Wide Web Consortium (W3C). WCAG defines requirements for making web content more accessible.

Howl is currently partially conformant with WCAG 2.2 Level AA. "Partially conformant" means that some parts of the platform do not yet fully meet the standard. We are actively working to close the gaps described in Section 5. We do not claim full conformance.

3. Scope

This statement applies to the Howl web application, the Howl desktop application (Windows, macOS, and Linux), and the Howl marketing website. Some areas rely on third-party or embedded components (for example, the GIF picker, payment forms, and the real-time voice and video call interface) whose accessibility we do not fully control; we work with these providers and account for their behavior where we can, but they may not meet the same standard as the rest of the Service.

4. Accessibility Features

Measures Howl currently provides include:

  • Reduced motion: a setting that minimizes animations and transitions, which also follows your operating system's "reduce motion" preference automatically.
  • Color and contrast options: a high-contrast mode, a color-saturation reduction control, and an "always underline links" option so links do not rely on color alone.
  • Animation controls: separate controls to stop autoplaying GIFs, animated emoji, and animated stickers.
  • Text-to-speech: an adjustable text-to-speech option with rate control.
  • Keyboard navigation: application-wide keyboard shortcuts, a "skip to main content" link to bypass the navigation sidebars, a visible focus indicator on interactive elements, and focus trapping within dialogs and modals so keyboard and screen-reader users do not lose their place.
  • Semantic markup and labels: ARIA roles, labels, and live regions throughout the interface, and meaningful alternative text on images.

5. Known Limitations

Despite our efforts, we are aware of areas that do not yet meet WCAG 2.2 Level AA. Known limitations include:

  • We have not yet completed a formal, comprehensive screen-reader audit across the entire product.
  • Some embedded and real-time surfaces (calls, media pickers) may not be fully accessible.

These items are on our roadmap. If you encounter a barrier that is not listed here, please tell us (Section 6).

6. Feedback and Contact

We welcome your feedback on the accessibility of Howl. If you encounter a barrier, or need information from Howl in a different format, contact us:

Email: support@howlpro.com (subject: "Accessibility")
Mailing Address: Howl LLC, 30 N Gould St Ste N, Sheridan, WY 82801, USA.

We aim to acknowledge accessibility feedback within five business days and will work with you to provide the information, feature, or service you need through an accessible alternative where a fix is not immediately available.

7. Compatibility with Assistive Technology

Howl is designed to work with current versions of major browsers (Chrome, Edge, Firefox, and Safari) and with the assistive technologies commonly used with them, including screen readers, screen magnification, speech-to-text, and operating-system accessibility settings such as reduced motion. Because we have not yet completed formal end-to-end screen-reader testing across the product, some combinations may work better than others; please report any problems so we can investigate.

8. Legal Framework

This statement is provided in the spirit of the Americans with Disabilities Act (ADA) and Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act (29 U.S.C. § 794d), which reference WCAG as the technical standard for digital accessibility. For users in the European Union and the United Kingdom, our WCAG 2.2 Level AA target aligns with EN 301 549 and the European Accessibility Act. This statement does not create rights or obligations beyond those required by applicable law.

9. Review

We review this statement and our accessibility practices periodically and after significant changes to the Service. This statement was last reviewed on the date shown above.
