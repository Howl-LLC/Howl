Howl Privacy Policy

Effective Date: March 4, 2026 · Last Updated: June 16, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Howl LLC ("Howl," "we," "us," "our") collects, uses, stores, and protects your personal information when you use the Howl platform, including our website, desktop application, mobile application, and all related services (the "Service"). By using the Service, you agree to the practices described in this policy.

If you have questions, contact us at support@howlpro.com.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide

  • Account information: Username, email address, password (securely hashed; we never store your password in plain text), and date of birth (used for age verification; see Section 10).
  • Profile information: Display name, avatar image, banner image, status text, and accent color.
  • Phone number: If you enable SMS two-factor authentication, we store your phone number (encrypted at rest). We use it only to send MFA codes.
  • Payment information: If you purchase a subscription, payment is processed by Stripe. We store your Stripe customer ID and subscription status but never see or store your full card number. See Stripe's Privacy Policy.
  • User content: Messages, images, files, and other content you send through servers and direct messages.
  • Communication preferences: Notification settings, privacy settings, language preference, and accessibility preferences.
  • Push notification subscriptions: If you enable push notifications, we store the subscription endpoint and cryptographic keys required to deliver notifications to your device. We do not receive delivery or interaction data from push services about individual notifications.

1.2 Information Collected Automatically

  • Usage data: Features you use, servers you join, channels you visit, and timestamps of activity. We use this to provide the Service (for example, showing online status), not for advertising or profiling.
  • Presence and activity indicators: Your online status (online, idle, do-not-disturb, offline). If you enable linked-account activity sharing (Spotify listening status, game presence, Twitch streaming, custom status), those activity indicators are shared with your contacts according to your Settings. You control which accounts you link and what activity, if any, is shared.
  • Device and connection data: IP address (for rate limiting, security, and abuse prevention), browser or client type, operating system, and device identifiers provided by Electron or your browser. The Electron desktop application periodically checks for updates by requesting a version manifest from our release server, which receives your IP address and client version. We store a truncated, salted hash of your IP on every login session to detect new-device sign-ins. We additionally store the raw IP address and full user-agent string on each session record for up to 90 days of inactivity for security and abuse-prevention investigations; both are then automatically nulled out. See Section 7 for retention details.
  • Upload safety hashing: When you upload an image, we compute a perceptual hash (PDQ) and a SHA-256 cryptographic hash of the image on our servers. Animated images (GIF, animated WebP, AVIF) are sampled at multiple frames so abuse cannot hide on a non-first frame. For non-image uploads (video, audio, documents), we compute a SHA-256 only. The PDQ hash is compared against a database of known harmful content (for example, child sexual abuse material) to block illegal uploads; SHA-256 is retained so that, if a file is later identified as illegal content, we can include the cross-provider exact-match hash in our reports to NCMEC and similar bodies. All hashing runs on our infrastructure, in memory, from the validated upload bytes; no upload data is sent to a third party for this purpose. End-to-end encrypted direct messages are excluded from this scanning because we cannot decrypt their contents.
  • Error reports: If you consent, we collect crash reports and performance data via Sentry. These reports are stripped of authentication headers and cookies before transmission. See Section 8 (Cookies and Tracking).

1.3 Information from Third Parties

  • Single sign-on (SSO): If you sign in with Google, Apple, or Steam, we receive your account identifier and email address from the SSO provider. We do not receive your password.
  • Linked gaming and social accounts: If you connect a third-party account (Riot Games, Epic Games, Steam, Twitch, YouTube, GitHub, Reddit, Spotify, and similar), we receive your account identifier, display name, and public profile data from that provider. Game stats (rank, match history) are fetched on your behalf and cached. You can disconnect linked accounts at any time in Settings.

We do not intentionally collect special categories of personal data under GDPR Article 9 or "sensitive personal information" under California's CPRA, including:

  • Racial or ethnic origin, political opinions, religious or philosophical beliefs, or trade-union membership;
  • Genetic data or biometric data used to uniquely identify a person;
  • Health data;
  • Data concerning sex life or sexual orientation;
  • Precise geolocation (our IP-derived location is approximate, not precise);
  • Social security numbers, driver's license numbers, passport numbers, financial account numbers, or similar government identifiers.

Please do not share such information through the Service. If you voluntarily share sensitive information in User Content (for example, inside a message), we process it as part of delivering the Service but do not derive inferences from it.

5. End-to-End Encrypted Direct Messages

Direct messages (DMs), group direct messages, and DM voice and video calls on Howl are end-to-end encrypted. Howl uses the Messaging Layer Security protocol (MLS, RFC 9420) for direct-message and group-DM content encryption and key management. With end-to-end encryption:

  • Message and call content is encrypted on your device and decrypted only on the recipient's device.
  • In Maximum Privacy mode (the default), we store only ciphertext and cannot read your encrypted DMs. If you opt into Secure & Easy mode, we hold a recoverable backup of your keys; see Section 5.2.
  • Metadata (sender, recipient, timestamp, channel ID, approximate message size) is not encrypted and is accessible to us for service operation and legal compliance.

5.1 Maximum Privacy mode (default)

Your encryption keys are derived from a passphrase you set and are stored only on your devices. Howl holds no copy of your passphrase or keys. If you lose both your passphrase and your recovery key, your E2E-encrypted data is permanently unrecoverable. We cannot reset your passphrase or recover your data for you.

5.2 Secure & Easy mode (opt-in)

Howl stores a password-protected backup of your encryption keys (an "escrow blob"), encrypted with a key derived from your account password. This allows recovery: after resetting your account password, you can complete the server-recovery flow from Settings → Encryption on your next login to regain access to your E2E-encrypted data. In this mode, Howl LLC and authorized personnel could, in principle, derive your encryption keys if compelled by valid legal process combined with access to your account password (a capability we do not use absent such process).

5.3 File Attachments

Files sent in encrypted DMs are encrypted on your device using AES-256-GCM with a unique per-file key before upload. Thumbnails are encrypted separately with their own keys. File encryption keys are distributed inside the end-to-end-encrypted message payload. In Maximum Privacy mode (the default) we never have access to them and cannot decrypt your files; if you opt into Secure & Easy mode, Howl could derive these keys under valid legal process, as described in Section 5.2. Encrypted files are stored with randomized filenames.

5.4 Reporting Secure DMs

If you report a message in a Secure (end-to-end encrypted) DM, your device shares the following with Howl's moderation team: the decrypted plaintext of the specific reported message and the encryption key for that conversation. Our moderation team may use the key to decrypt surrounding messages in that conversation for context. Your other conversations are not decrypted or reviewed as part of the report. Shared content is encrypted at rest on our servers using a separate server-side key and is used only for moderation review.

5.5 Local Plaintext Cache

To avoid re-decrypting messages on every page load, your device caches decrypted DM content locally (in your browser's IndexedDB). This cache is scoped to your account, is not shared with us, and is cleared when you log out. You can manually clear it at any time in Account Settings > Data Controls.

6. Voice and Video Communications

Howl does not record or store the audio or video content of voice or video calls.

  • Server voice channels use SFrame end-to-end encryption between participants, carried over a DTLS-SRTP transport. The per-channel key is generated and distributed among the participants' devices, so our media servers forward the encrypted frames for routing without access to the media content. Server voice channel media is not recorded or retained.
  • Direct-message voice and video calls use SFrame end-to-end encryption between participants. The per-call encryption key is derived from the conversation's end-to-end-encrypted (MLS) group state and is available only to the participants' devices. Our media servers forward the encrypted frames without access to the media content.

Metadata about calls (participant identifiers, start and end timestamps, channel ID, approximate duration) is visible to our servers for service operation and abuse prevention. If you report an incident that occurred in a call, reports may rely on participant-submitted evidence since we do not retain media.

7. Data Retention

  • Account data: Retained as long as your account is active.
  • Server messages: Per server retention settings (configured by the server owner). If no limit is set, messages are retained until deleted by the server owner or message author.
  • Direct messages: Retained until you or the other participant deletes them, or until you delete your account.
  • Audit logs: Server audit logs are retained for 90 days by default, or as configured by the server owner.
  • Sessions: Login sessions are automatically deleted after 90 days of inactivity.
  • Raw IP address and user-agent on sessions: Stored on the session record at sign-in for security and abuse-prevention investigations; automatically nulled out after 90 days of inactivity. The truncated, salted IP hash used for new-device detection is retained for the lifetime of the session record itself.
  • Push notification subscriptions: Automatically removed after 90 days.
  • Data exports: Download files are available for 14 days, then permanently deleted.
  • Temporary data: Email verification codes (15 minutes), password reset codes (expiry-based), and expired invites are automatically cleaned up.
  • Upload safety hashes: Perceptual (PDQ; image content only) and cryptographic (SHA-256; all non-encrypted uploads, including video, audio, and documents) hashes are retained for 180 days for safety auditing. Hashes that match known harmful content are retained indefinitely for legal compliance.
  • Child-safety evidence (CSAM): Where we identify content as child sexual abuse material, whether by hash match at upload time, by user report and Trust & Safety review, or by a National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) hash list, we preserve the file, hashes, message and account metadata, the raw IP address and user-agent associated with the upload, and a snapshot of the uploader's profile (username, email hash, account creation date) for as long as required under 18 U.S.C. § 2258A and related law. This preservation overrides our normal deletion timelines, including account self-deletion: a user who deletes their account does not erase confirmed CSAM evidence.
  • Error reports (Sentry): Retained per Sentry's data retention policy (typically 90 days).
  • Account deletion: Profile, email, and settings are permanently deleted. Messages are anonymized (author replaced with "Deleted User"). Uploaded files are deleted from storage. Some data is retained where required by law, including active legal proceedings and CSAM evidence preservation as described above.

9.6 Automated Decision-Making

We do not make decisions about you based solely on automated processing that produce legal effects or similarly significant effects. Some moderation features use automated techniques:

  • Spam and abuse detection. Automated systems may temporarily restrict actions (for example, message rate limits) based on behavioral patterns. These restrictions are short-lived and reversible.
  • Image safety scanning. Uploads are scanned using perceptual hashing against a database of known harmful content (CSAM). Matches may block the upload and be reported to the appropriate authorities. See Section 2.
  • Server moderation rules. Server owners may configure keyword, link, spam, and mention filters within their own communities. These apply only to servers you participate in and are controlled by the server owner, not Howl.

All automated actions can be appealed by contacting support@howlpro.com with the subject "Account Appeal". A human will review your case, and the decision may be reversed. Under GDPR Article 22 and similar laws, you have the right to human review of any decision that has a legal or similarly significant effect.

10. Children's Privacy

Howl is for users aged 13 and older. We do not knowingly collect data from children under 13. If we learn we have, we will promptly delete it and terminate the account. Users between 13 and 17 may use the Service with parental or guardian consent. At registration, users in this age range must affirmatively confirm, via a checkbox separate from the Terms of Service agreement, that a parent or legal guardian has read these Terms and consents to their use of Howl. We record this affirmation on the user's account. We do not currently verify parental consent through a parent-side challenge; the affirmation is an attestation, consistent with standard industry practice for general-audience communication platforms. We additionally offer Family Link tools that allow parents to manage their child's account. Family Link is optional. Contact support@howlpro.com if you believe a child under 13 is using the Service.
