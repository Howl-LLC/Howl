Howl Community Guidelines

Effective Date: April 20, 2026 · Last Updated: April 20, 2026

Howl is a place to hang out with friends, build communities, play games together, and have real conversations. These Guidelines exist so everyone can enjoy that.

These Guidelines apply everywhere on Howl: servers, direct messages, group DMs, voice and video calls, Stages, profiles, custom emoji and stickers, and everything else you can do on the platform. They are incorporated by reference into our Terms of Service and explain what is and is not allowed.

By using Howl, you agree to follow these Guidelines. Server owners and moderators can set additional rules in their own servers on top of these, but they cannot override or relax platform-wide policy.

Context and intent matter. Journalism, documentary, satire, education, counter-speech, and clear condemnation of harmful content are not the same thing as promoting it. When we enforce these Guidelines, we consider the framing, audience, and apparent intent of the content, not only the words or images themselves. Content that would otherwise be prohibited may be allowed when it serves a clear public-interest purpose and is presented with appropriate context, labeling, and age-gating where needed. Context is a factor in interpretation; it is not a pass for hate, harassment, CSAM, or incitement.

1. Respect Each Other

1.1 No Harassment or Bullying

Do not target, harass, bully, or intimidate other users. This includes:

  • Unwanted repeated contact, including continuing to message someone after they have asked you to stop or blocked you.
  • Organizing or participating in "raids" or other coordinated campaigns to brigade, spam, or harass a user, server, or community.
  • Encouraging others to harass a specific person (piling on).
  • Sexual harassment or unwanted sexual messages.
  • Mocking, demeaning, or belittling someone because of a personal trauma, identity, or vulnerability.

1.2 No Threats of Violence

Do not threaten, encourage, or promote violence against a person or group. This covers direct threats ("I will hurt you"), indirect threats ("something bad is going to happen to you"), conditional threats, and threats framed as jokes or "irony".

1.3 No Doxxing or Privacy Violations

Do not share someone else's private information without their consent, including home address, workplace, phone number, government-issued IDs, financial details, medical records, or other personally identifiable information. This includes operating or using services that compile, sell, or distribute such information.

1.4 No Hate Speech or Discrimination

Do not attack, demean, or promote hatred against people based on protected characteristics, including race, ethnicity, national origin, caste, color, immigration status, religion, sex, gender identity or expression, sexual orientation, age, disability, or serious disease. Hate symbols, slurs, imagery, and denial or minimization of genocide and other mass atrocities are not allowed.

1.5 No Violent Extremism or Terrorism

Do not organize, promote, glorify, coordinate, or participate in acts of violence or violent extremism. This covers terrorist organizations, organized violent hate groups, violent conspiracy movements, and individuals who have committed mass violence. Celebrating, memorializing, or recruiting for such groups and acts is prohibited.

2. Protect Minors

Howl has zero tolerance for content and behavior that endangers minors. This is the most strictly enforced section of these Guidelines. Violations will result in immediate account termination and reporting to the appropriate authorities.

2.1 Child Sexual Abuse Material (CSAM)

Do not post, share, link to, solicit, or trade any child sexual abuse material. This applies to all forms, including:

  • Photographs and videos of real children.
  • Artistic depictions, drawings, animations, or computer-generated imagery of minors in sexual contexts.
  • Synthetic or AI-generated content involving real or fictional minors.
  • Text descriptions of sexual activity involving minors.
  • Technical prompts, datasets, or tools produced to generate such content.

We report CSAM to the National Center for Missing & Exploited Children (NCMEC) through CyberTipline, as required by U.S. law.

2.2 Teen Safety

If you are under 18, do not engage in sexual conduct or post sexually explicit content on Howl. Do not use Howl to arrange romantic or sexual interactions with other minors, and do not run or join spaces intended to facilitate that.

2.3 Adult Interactions with Minors

Adults must not engage in, solicit, groom, coerce, or exchange sexual or romantic content with anyone under 18. Adults must not engage in romantic or sexual communication with minors. Violations are treated as CSAM equivalents and reported to authorities.

2.4 Endangerment

Do not knowingly expose minors to content or interactions that pose a serious risk to their physical or emotional safety.

3. Adult Content

3.1 Sexually Explicit Content

Sexually explicit content is allowed only:

  • In channels that the server owner has explicitly labeled as age-restricted (NSFW).
  • Between consenting adults in direct messages.

It is never allowed in avatars, banners, usernames, status, profile bios, server icons, server banners, invite splash images, custom emoji, stickers, or soundboard clips. Users must be 18 or older to view age-restricted content. If you encounter what appears to be a minor in an NSFW context, stop and report immediately.

3.2 Non-Consensual Intimate Imagery

Do not share, post, or threaten to share intimate or sexual images or videos of a person without their explicit, informed, and ongoing consent. This includes:

  • Images or recordings created without the subject's knowledge.
  • Images originally made with consent but shared beyond the agreed audience ("revenge porn").
  • Synthetic or AI-generated nude or sexual imagery of a real person ("deepfake nudes").
  • Requests for, or trade in, such content.

4. Self-Harm and Violence

4.1 Self-Harm, Suicide, and Eating Disorders

Do not promote, glorify, encourage, or provide instructions for self-harm, suicide, or eating disorders. Do not use threats of self-harm to manipulate other people.

We want Howl to be a place where people can talk about mental health, recovery, and hard experiences with care. Content that supports and informs (for example, peer support, recovery stories, crisis-line information) is not the same as content that encourages harm. If you or someone you know is in crisis, contact local emergency services first; Howl is not an emergency service.

4.2 Violence and Gore

Do not post gratuitous depictions of violence, injury, torture, animal cruelty, death, or gore, especially when intended to shock, glorify, or harass. Documentary, educational, news, or historical content addressing violence may be permitted with appropriate context and age-gating.

5. Keep Howl Safe

5.1 Spam and Automation

Do not send unsolicited mass messages, inflate engagement, or run bots pretending to be humans. This covers:

  • Spam direct messages, mentions, or invites.
  • Tools and services that manufacture accounts, solve CAPTCHAs, generate tokens, or organize raids.
  • Bot and automation accounts operating without the server owner's consent and outside our developer policy.
  • Selling or buying artificial activity (follower counts, boost numbers, engagement).

5.2 Account and Asset Trading

You may not sell, trade, lease, share, or buy Howl accounts, usernames, vanity URLs, server ownership, server permissions, or invite links. One human, one account.

5.3 Evading Enforcement

Do not create new accounts, servers, or communities to evade warnings, suspensions, bans, or permission losses. Evasion is itself a serious violation and can extend the original enforcement.

5.4 Security Threats

Do not use Howl to compromise accounts, devices, or networks. This includes:

  • Phishing, credential theft, or impersonating Howl or third parties to steal information.
  • Distributing malware, remote-access tools, or exploit kits.
  • Denial-of-service attacks, coordinated account lockouts, or token exhaustion.
  • Unauthorized access to any Howl system or another user's account.

Security researchers acting in good faith should follow our responsible-disclosure process described in the Terms of Service Section on Security and Vulnerability Disclosure.

5.5 Financial Scams and Fraud

Do not use Howl to defraud others or promote financial scams, including:

  • Investment or cryptocurrency scams, "pump and dump" coordination, rug pulls.
  • Phishing or identity-theft schemes.
  • Counterfeit goods, forged documents, stolen credentials or accounts.
  • Money laundering, price gouging, or money-mule recruitment.

5.6 Impersonation and Inauthentic Identity

Do not impersonate Howl staff, law enforcement, public figures, or other users in a way that is intended to deceive. Fan accounts and parody are allowed when clearly labeled as such.

5.7 Misinformation with Real-World Harm

Do not post false or misleading information that could cause serious physical harm, disrupt critical infrastructure, obstruct participation in civic processes, or endanger public health. Opinion, satire, commentary, and discussion of disputed topics are not automatically misinformation; the test is real-world harm, not disagreement.

5.8 False Reports

Do not submit false, exaggerated, or coordinated abuse reports to harass another user, shut down a server, or waste moderation resources. Pattern abuse of the reporting system is itself a violation.

6. Follow the Law

6.1 Intellectual Property

Do not post or share content that infringes someone else's copyright, trademark, or other intellectual-property rights. Do not operate spaces that distribute pirated media, DRM-bypassing tools, game cheats or hacks sold for profit, or credential-sharing services. Report infringement through our Copyright and IP Policy.

6.2 Regulated Goods

Do not sell, trade, gift, or coordinate the exchange of regulated or dangerous goods that could cause physical harm, including firearms and firearm components, explosives, drugs (illegal or prescription), pharmaceuticals, or counterfeit goods.

6.3 Gambling

Do not run or participate in illegal gambling activity on Howl. If you operate a gambling service that is legal in your jurisdiction, you are still responsible for compliance with all applicable laws, age restrictions, and advertising rules.

6.4 Other Illegal Activity

Do not use Howl to coordinate, promote, or execute criminal activity, including human trafficking, commercial sexual solicitation, theft, or violence.

7. Commerce and Promotion

People can sell legal products or services, accept tips and donations, run paid communities, and promote their server or event on Howl. That activity still has to follow these Guidelines and the law.

7.1 Legal Commerce

If you sell or trade goods or services on Howl:

  • Follow applicable consumer-protection, advertising, tax, and licensing laws in the jurisdictions where you and your customers are located.
  • Be transparent about what you are selling, the price, delivery or access terms, and who is responsible.
  • Do not sell, promote, or accept payment for anything prohibited elsewhere in these Guidelines (for example, CSAM, weapons, non-consensual intimate imagery, or account access).
  • Honor the agreements you make with customers. Taking payment and failing to deliver, or materially misrepresenting a product, is a violation and may be fraud under applicable law.

7.2 Tips, Donations, and Community Support

Asking for tips, donations, or community support is allowed if:

  • You do not misrepresent who the money is for or how it will be used.
  • You do not pressure, shame, or coerce members into donating. Locking basic community participation behind payment, guilt-tripping members, or threatening removal for non-payment is prohibited.
  • You comply with fundraising, charity, and solicitation laws if you are raising money on behalf of a person, cause, or organization.

7.3 Pyramid, MLM, and Referral Schemes

Do not use Howl to recruit into:

  • Pyramid schemes, chain-recruitment "business opportunities", or matrix schemes whose primary mechanic is recruiting new participants.
  • Multi-level marketing (MLM) recruitment that prioritizes downline recruitment over genuine product sales.
  • "Get rich quick" programs that promise fixed or guaranteed returns without proportionate risk disclosure.

7.4 Investment and Financial Advice

If you offer investment, cryptocurrency, or financial advice on Howl, you are responsible for complying with securities, commodities, and financial-services laws in your jurisdiction, including any licensing, disclosure, and suitability requirements. Coordinated market-manipulation behavior (pump-and-dump, wash trading, and similar) is prohibited under Section 5.5.

7.5 Promoting Your Server or Community

You can advertise your server, promote events, and invite people to join. You may not do so through:

  • Unsolicited direct messages to users who have not interacted with you or your community.
  • Spam replies, @-mentions, or bulk invite-blasting across servers.
  • Raid-style promotion campaigns in unrelated communities.
  • Fake engagement, paid-for member counts, or purchased boost volume.

8. End-to-End Encryption Responsibilities

Howl supports end-to-end encryption for Secure direct messages, group direct messages, and DM voice and video calls. These Guidelines apply inside encrypted conversations even though we cannot see the content.

  • If you receive abusive, illegal, or prohibited content in an encrypted DM, you can report it from within the app. Reporting shares the decrypted content and key for that conversation with our moderation team. See the Privacy Policy for details about how reports are handled.
  • CSAM detection applies to uploaded images regardless of where they are posted. Image uploads are hashed and compared against known harmful content on our servers before delivery; confirmed matches are reported to NCMEC.
  • People who use E2E to coordinate serious harm that comes to our attention will face the same enforcement as anyone else, including termination and law-enforcement referral where appropriate.

End-to-end encryption protects privacy. It is not a safe haven for abuse.

9. Voice, Video, and Stages

The same rules apply in voice channels, video calls, screen shares, and Stages as in text channels, with a few extras:

  • Obtain consent from every participant before recording a call. Non-consensual recording may violate wiretapping and privacy laws in your jurisdiction.
  • Do not broadcast content that violates these Guidelines during a screen share or camera stream.
  • Speakers and hosts in a Stage are responsible for what is said on their stage. Repeated Guideline violations can result in loss of stage privileges or server-wide enforcement.
  • Do not use voice or video to bypass text-channel moderation (for example, saying out loud what you cannot type).

10. Server Owners and Moderators

If you run a server on Howl, you are responsible for keeping it in line with these Guidelines. That means:

  • Setting server rules that do not conflict with these Guidelines.
  • Removing prohibited content and members when you find them.
  • Not advertising your server through spam, raids, fake engagement, or unauthorized DMs.
  • Not using moderation tools, bans, or private channels to harass, dox, or stalk members.
  • Not monetizing or profiting from prohibited content (through boosts, gifts, off-platform donations, or any other channel).
  • Making sure any custom bots, webhooks, or automations you add to your server also follow these Guidelines and our developer policies.

Server owners who tolerate or encourage repeated Guideline violations can lose their server, even if they did not personally post the offending content.

11. Reporting Violations

Howl relies on users and server moderators to flag problems. You can report in the following ways:

  • In the app: right-click or long-press a message, user, or server and choose Report.
  • By email: support@howlpro.com with a description and any available message links, message IDs, or user IDs.
  • Imminent, real-world threat to life: contact local emergency services first (911 in the United States, 999 in the United Kingdom, 112 in most of the European Union, or the equivalent in your jurisdiction). Howl cannot dispatch emergency responders.

Please do not take matters into your own hands. Vigilante activity (raiding, doxxing, "justice" pile-ons) often puts innocent people at risk and usually violates these Guidelines regardless of the alleged provocation.

See our Privacy Policy for how reports are handled, including how E2E content is shared when you report an encrypted message.

12. Enforcement

We review reports and, when we confirm a violation, we may take one or more of the following actions:

  • Issue a warning.
  • Remove the offending content.
  • Temporarily restrict account or server features (for example, revoking the ability to send DMs, join voice channels, or create servers).
  • Suspend the account or server.
  • Permanently terminate the account or server and ban the responsible user(s).
  • Report the conduct to NCMEC, law enforcement, or other appropriate authorities.

We weigh the severity of the violation, whether it is a repeat, the harm caused, and off-platform behavior that indicates a credible threat to users. Off-platform behavior (for example, coordinated raids planned elsewhere) can factor into Howl-side enforcement.

We may act against content or accounts that violate the spirit of these Guidelines, even when the specific behavior is not explicitly listed. If something is clearly harmful, hateful, deceptive, illegal, or dangerous, we will address it.

12.1 Appeals

If you believe an enforcement action was mistaken, you can appeal. See the Appeals section of our Terms of Service for the process and time limits.

12.2 Evading Enforcement

Creating new accounts or identities to return after a ban, or encouraging others to do the same on your behalf, is an additional violation and can extend the original enforcement.

13. Changes

We may update these Guidelines as Howl grows and as new kinds of harm appear. Material changes will be announced through the Service or by email. Your continued use of Howl after a change takes effect means you accept the updated Guidelines.

If you have questions about these Guidelines, contact us at support@howlpro.com.
